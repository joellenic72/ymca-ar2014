Headline?

Did you know that there are 495,683 people living below the poverty line in metropolitan Washington? In addition to their dire financial needs, the Y is here day-in and day-out to provide the resources our community needs to address the most pressing social issues: child welfare, education, employment, counseling, citizenship services and more. We work to make sure every child, family and community has what they need to achieve their best.

Statistics

Headline?

YMCAs have a history of serving their communities in resourceful and innovative ways. Our staff and volunteers may not have all the answers, but here’s how their courage challenged personal and social ills in 2014.

$2,662,507 was provided in scholarships and subsidies to 12,498 children and adults who turned to the Y for help. From membership to youth sports, summer camp to swim lessons, and so much more, the Y was accessible to all.

3,142 volunteers gave 161,572 hours of their time and talent to help the YMCA fulfill its mission.

25.39% of the overnight campers at YMCA Camp Letts received scholarships to help cover the cost of their summer camp experience.

3,700 adults and children were served by YMCA Youth and Family Services (YFS). YFS services are free of charge and include counseling, mentoring, and early intervention for Montgomery County’s neediest families.

We hosted 49 interns from Catholic, George Washington, Loyola, Marymount, Michigan State, Old Dominion and many more Universities, helping them develop professional and career-related skills.
